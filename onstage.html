Ideathon

  • 3 participants per team, 1 team per department/institution.
  • Each team will have a maximum of 10 slides to present their idea, along with 5 minutes for presentation and 2 minutes for a Q&A session.
  • Presentations should focus on the idea’s impact, sustainability, and scalability.
  • Use of offensive or inappropriate content will result in disqualification.
  • Time limits and the 10-slide maximum must be strictly adhered to; Strictly adhere to the time limit & no. of slides.
  • Judges’ decisions regarding originality, feasibility, and presentation quality will be final.
  • The team with the most innovative and well-presented idea and meet the sustainable development goals will be declared the winner.

Connections

  • 3 participants per team, 1 team per department/institution.
  • The game will consist of multiple rounds where the participants will be given sets of related words or concepts.
  • Teams must identify the connection between the given words or concepts and give the final answer within the time limit.
  • Teams must discuss and give their final answer within the time limit.
  • No external help or devices are allowed during the competition.
  • Each correct answer earns points, and incorrect answers will not be penalized.
  • Judges’ decisions on connections will be final and binding.
  • The team with the highest total score across all rounds will be declared the winner.

Heads Up

  • 2 participants per team, 1 team per department/institution.
  • One player will guess the word displayed on the board, while the other gives indirect verbal clues to help identify the word.
  • Use of sentences as clues or gestures will lead to disqualification.
  • Players can say "PASS" to skip a word.
  • The team that guesses the most words correctly wins.
  • Judge's decision will be final.

Debate

  • 3 participants per team, 1 team per department/institution.
  • Topics will be provided 30 minutes prior to the start of the debate.
  • Each team will have 5 minutes for preparation and 10 minutes to present their arguments.
  • Teams must present arguments both for and against the given topic.
  • Offensive language or personal attacks will result in disqualification.
  • Teams must bring their own materials (e.g., notebooks, pens) for note-taking.
  • Participants are encouraged to present data and facts to support their arguments.
  • Interruptions during the opponent's presentation are not allowed; time will be provided for rebuttals.
  • Time limits must be strictly adhered to; exceeding the limit will result in point deductions.
  • Decisions made by the judging panel are final and binding.
  • The team with the most points based on content, clarity, delivery, and rebuttal wins.
